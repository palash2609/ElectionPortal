Voter Guidelines for Indian Elections

  • Registered Voter

    Ensure you are registered as a voter before the election day. If not, register yourself as soon as possible.

  • Check Electoral Roll

    Check your name on the electoral roll to ensure that your name is listed correctly. You can check this information online or at your nearest election office.

  • Carry Voter ID

    Carry your Voter ID card to the polling station as it serves as a valid identity proof for voting. In case you don't have a Voter ID card, you can use other alternative ID proofs specified by the Election Commission.

  • Verify Candidates

    Before voting, verify the candidates and parties contesting in your constituency. Understand their agendas and promises to make an informed decision.

  • Avoid Malpractice

    Do not engage in any form of electoral malpractice such as bribing voters, booth capturing, or any other illegal activities. Voting is a right and should be exercised freely and fairly.

  • Follow Instructions

    Follow the instructions given by election officials at the polling station. Cooperate with them for a smooth voting process.

  • No Campaign Material

    Do not carry any campaign material such as pamphlets, banners, or posters inside the polling station. Maintain the decorum of the election process.

  • No Selfies

    Do not take selfies inside the polling booth. It is against the rules of the Election Commission and can lead to disqualification of your vote.

  • Vote Responsibly

    Cast your vote responsibly after careful consideration of the candidates and their agendas. Your vote matters in shaping the future of the country.

  • Keep ID Safe

    Keep your Voter ID card safe after voting. It may be required for future references and serves as an important document for various purposes.
