BJP (Bharatiya Janata Party)

The Bharatiya Janata Party is one of the two major political parties in India, along with the Indian National Congress. It is the current ruling party in the country.

Key Points:

  • Founded: 1980
  • Ideology: Hindutva, Nationalism, Social Conservatism
  • Current Leader: Narendra Modi
  • Seats in Lok Sabha (as of 2024): Majority
  • Significant Presence: Nationwide

Congress (Indian National Congress)

The Indian National Congress is one of the two major political parties in India, along with the Bharatiya Janata Party. It has a significant presence across the country.

Key Points:

  • Founded: 1885
  • Ideology: Social Democracy, Secularism
  • Current Leader: Sonia Gandhi (Interim)
  • Seats in Lok Sabha (as of 2024): Opposition
  • Significant Presence: Nationwide

AAP (Aam Aadmi Party)

The Aam Aadmi Party is a relatively new political party in India that emerged out of the anti-corruption movement. It has gained prominence, particularly in Delhi.

Key Points:

  • Founded: 2012
  • Ideology: Anti-Corruption, Populism
  • Current Leader: Arvind Kejriwal
  • Seats in Lok Sabha (as of 2024): Varied
  • Significant Presence: Delhi, Some Other States

BSP (Bahujan Samaj Party)

The Bahujan Samaj Party primarily represents the Bahujan Samaj, which includes Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, and Other Backward Classes. It has a significant presence in Uttar Pradesh.

Key Points:

  • Founded: 1984
  • Ideology: Ambedkarism, Social Equality
  • Current Leader: Mayawati
  • Seats in Lok Sabha (as of 2024): Varied
  • Significant Presence: Uttar Pradesh

SP (Samajwadi Party)

The Samajwadi Party is a regional political party in India, primarily based in the state of Uttar Pradesh. It represents the interests of the socialist section of society.

Key Points:

  • Founded: 1992
  • Ideology: Socialism, Secularism
  • Current Leader: Akhilesh Yadav
  • Seats in Lok Sabha (as of 2024): Varied
  • Significant Presence: Uttar Pradesh

AIADMK (All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam)

The AIADMK is a regional political party in the state of Tamil Nadu and Puducherry. It has a significant presence in the Tamil Nadu political landscape.

Key Points:

  • Founded: 1972
  • Ideology: Dravidian Nationalism, Social Democracy
  • Current Leader: O. Panneerselvam
  • Seats in Lok Sabha (as of 2024): Varied
  • Significant Presence: Tamil Nadu, Puducherry

DMK (Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam)

The Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam is a major political party in the state of Tamil Nadu and Puducherry. It represents the Dravidian people's interests.

Key Points:

  • Founded: 1949
  • Ideology: Dravidian Nationalism, Social Democracy
  • Current Leader: M. K. Stalin
  • Seats in Lok Sabha (as of 2024): Varied
  • Significant Presence: Tamil Nadu, Puducherry

CPI(M) (Communist Party of India (Marxist))

The Communist Party of India (Marxist) is a communist party in India. It is one of the major political parties in Kerala and has a presence in some other states as well.

Key Points:

  • Founded: 1964
  • Ideology: Communism, Marxism, Leninism
  • Current Leader: Sitaram Yechury
  • Seats in Lok Sabha (as of 2024): Varied
  • Significant Presence: Kerala, Some Other States
