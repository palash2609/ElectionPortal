About Election Commission of India

The Election Commission of India (ECI) is an autonomous constitutional authority established under the Constitution of India. It is responsible for administering election processes in the country and ensuring free, fair, and credible elections.

The ECI consists of a Chief Election Commissioner (CEC) and Election Commissioners. They are appointed by the President of India.

The primary objective of the ECI is to conduct elections for the Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha, State Legislative Assemblies, and State Legislative Councils in accordance with the principles of democracy and rule of law.

Structure

The Election Commission of India is headed by the Chief Election Commissioner (CEC). Currently, there are two Election Commissioners serving alongside the CEC.

The Chief Election Commissioner and the Election Commissioners are appointed by the President of India and hold office for a term of six years or until they attain the age of 65, whichever is earlier.

Chief Election Commissioner

Mr. Sunil Arora

Mr. Sunil Arora is the current Chief Election Commissioner of India. He assumed office on 2nd December 2018. Prior to his appointment as CEC, Mr. Arora has served in various capacities in the Indian Administrative Service (IAS).

As the Chief Election Commissioner, Mr. Sunil Arora oversees the conduct of elections across the country and ensures the integrity and fairness of the electoral process.

Functions and Responsibilities

  • Conducting free, fair, and impartial elections at all levels of government.
  • Delimitation of constituencies for the purpose of elections to ensure fair representation.
  • Registration of political parties and allocation of election symbols.
  • Monitoring election expenditure of candidates and political parties to prevent corruption and malpractices.
  • Implementing the Model Code of Conduct to ensure fair campaigning during elections.
  • Overseeing the enforcement of electoral laws and regulations.
  • Conducting voter education programs to enhance awareness and participation in the electoral process.
  • Managing the electoral roll and ensuring the integrity of voter registration.
  • Addressing complaints and grievances related to the electoral process.
  • Providing transparency in the election process through various initiatives.
